Project Two

Eventbrite Wallet — Reducing Queues and Unlocking In-Event Revenue

A product concept that expands Eventbrite beyond ticketing by introducing a digital wallet for food, beverages and event services. The solution reduces purchase friction, improves attendee experience and creates a new revenue opportunity for organizers and the platform.

Overview

Events are meant to be lived, not waited for. But in many live experiences, attendees lose valuable time in queues while organizers lose revenue due to limited service capacity, poor demand predictability and abandoned purchases.

The opportunity was to expand Eventbrite’s role beyond ticketing by supporting the full event consumption journey. The proposed solution introduces a digital wallet inside the Eventbrite app, allowing users to buy in advance or during the event and redeem purchases with less friction.

Process

The design approach focused on two core journeys: pre-event purchase and in-event purchase. Before the event, users can buy tickets and add drinks or services in advance. During the event, they can purchase in real time, show a QR code and quickly return to the experience.

The main design challenge was speed. In an event context, every extra step increases friction. Instead of reinventing event consumption, the concept improves behaviors that already exist: buying, paying and redeeming.
