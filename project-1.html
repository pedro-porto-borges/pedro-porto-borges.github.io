Project One

MOVA: Optimizing the In-Store Installment Financing Journey

A professional case focused on improving an assisted sales flow used by store operators to offer financial products in a retail environment. The work combined journey mapping, behavioral funnel analysis, operator research, business rules and cross-functional collaboration to simplify a complex financial decision.

Overview

In Brazil, department store credit cards operate as more than payment instruments. They are connected to credit, insurance, loyalty and financial services. Inside this ecosystem, the store operator plays a key role: explaining the offer, handling objections and helping the customer make a real-time financial decision.

The problem was not just interface complexity. It was a B2B2C service experience involving the customer, the operator, the retailer and the bank. The journey needed to become simpler without losing trust, clarity or control.

How might we make the installment payment flow simpler and more efficient for operators, without losing clarity for customers in a debt-restructuring moment?

Process

I contributed across the research, analysis and journey optimization process. My work included mapping the physical journey inside Magalu stores and the digital journey inside the assisted channel, using behavioral funnel analysis to identify friction points and observing how operators interacted with the platform in real sales contexts.

I also supported stakeholder interviews, business rules mapping and cross-functional alignment with Product, BI, Design and Commercial teams. The solution focused on reducing unnecessary steps, improving clarity in key decision moments and aligning the interface with the reality of assisted sales.
